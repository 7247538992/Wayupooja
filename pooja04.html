Rudrabhishek with Curd
with Curd

is a sacred ritual that has been performed since ancient times. In this traditional practice, the Shivling is bathed using curd. From the Vedic era to the present day, devotees have been offering curd to Lord Shiva as a mark of deep faith and devotion. This form of abhishek is a simple yet powerful way to please and honor Lord Shiva.

Procedure Benefits Guideline

Procedure
Lighting the Lamp:

The ceremony begins with lighting a lamp in the devotee’s name, purifying the surroundings and invoking a divine presence.

Sankalp:

Panditji takes a Sankalp on behalf of the devotee, mentioning their name, gotra, and specific wishes.

Process:

The Shivling is bathed with Holy water while chanting Vedic and Rudra mantras, inviting divine energy.

Chanting & Prayers:

Along with mantra chanting, the devotee’s prayers and desires are offered to Lord Shiva, creating a spiritually uplifting atmosphere.

Offerings:

Sacred items like bel leaves, coconut, flowers and rice grains (akshat) are offered — all dearly loved by Lord Shiva.

Shivling Sringar:

The Shivling is beautifully decorated, enhancing the divine experience.

Aarti:

The ritual concludes with an Aarti, filling the space with sacred vibrations and connecting the devotee directly with Lord Shiva.

Time Duration:

Rudrabhishek will take approximately 15–20 minutes to complete, as it is performed with full devotion, detailed rituals, and utmost adherence to Vedic traditions.

This entire ritual brings divine blessings, inner peace, and spiritual fulfillment.



Benefits

Brings blessings for childbirth and family growth.

Promotes harmony and joy in married life.

Aids in success related to home, vehicle, and property matters.



Pooja Guideline
Sankalp

Sankalp is the most important part of any pooja. It includes your name, Gotra, and specific wishes, and is taken by our Panditji to ensure the spiritual benefits reach you. We follow true Vedic tradition, one Sankalp for one pooja at a time. We do not perform group Sankalps, as it goes against scriptural principles. Each pooja is done individually, with full focus and devotion.

Gotra

Gotra is not just a surname — it represents your ancestral lineage and connects your prayers to the Rishis and your entire family. If someone is unaware of their gotra, the “Kashyap Gotra” will be used.

Pooja Proof

We provide photos or short videos of key pooja moments like Sankalp or Abhishek, usually within 2 days. In rare cases, if proof cannot be shared, your official Temple Trust receipt is confirmation.

Pooja Schedule

Your payment is verified within 24 hours. After verification, the pooja is performed within the next 48 hours.

Pooja Samagri

All materials are arranged by our temple pandit as per traditional rituals. Alternatives are used if something is unavailable.

Prasad

No physical prasad is sent after the pooja.
