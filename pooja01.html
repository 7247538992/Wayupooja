Purna Rudrabhishek

Rudrabhishek means “Abhishek (sacred bathing) of Rudra.” Rudra is one of the names of Lord Shiva. This ritual is performed by devotees of Lord Shiva to seek his divine blessings. During Rudrabhishek, the Shivling is bathed with pure substances like water, milk, curd, ghee, honey, etc. While performing the ritual, sacred Shiva mantras are chanted, through which devotees seek prosperity, happiness, peace, and blessings in their lives. This is considered a highly auspicious ritual and a way to express devotion to Lord Shiva.

Procedure Benefits Guidelines

Procedure
Lighting of the Lamp:

A lamp is lit in the devotee’s name, purifying the environment and invoking divine presence.

Sankalp:
The pandit performs Sankalp by stating the devotee's name, Gotra, and specific wishes, signifying the devotee's sincere intent.

Process:
The Shivling is bathed with a mixture of sacred items like milk, curd, ghee, honey, perfume, Ganga Jal, and more, while chanting Vedic and Rudra mantras.

Chanting and Prayer:
Personal prayers and intentions are offered to Lord Shiva through sacred chanting, creating a deeply spiritual atmosphere.

Offerings:
Bel leaves, coconuts, flowers, and rice grains (Akshat) are offered with devotion.

Shivling Sringar:
The Shivling is adorned beautifully, creating a divine visual experience.

Havan:
A sacred fire ritual is conducted, offering to Lord Shiva, Parvati, Ganesha, Kartikeya, Nandi, the Shiva family, and Navagrahas to invoke blessings and pacify planetary doshas.

Aarti:
The pooja concludes with a grand Aarti, filling the environment with sacred energy and connecting the devotee to Lord Shiva’s divine vibrations.

Duration:
The entire ritual takes approximately 3 hours to complete with full devotion and adherence to Vedic traditions.

Difference Between Purna Rudrabhishek and Rudrabhishek
Duration:

Purna Rudrabhishek takes approximately 3 hours to complete, whereas Rudrabhishek is a shorter ritual, completed in about 30 minutes.

Number of Pandits:

Purna Rudrabhishek is performed by a total of 5 pandits, while Rudrabhishek is conducted by 2 pandits.

Number of Ritual Items (Samagri):

Purna Rudrabhishek uses 21 types of traditional ritual items, whereas Rudrabhishek uses 11 items.

Mantra Chanting and Archana:

Purna Rudrabhishek involves more extensive mantra chanting and detailed Archana (ritual worship), while Rudrabhishek includes fewer mantras and a brief Archana.

Inclusion of Havan:

Purna Rudrabhishek includes a properly conducted Havan, whereas Rudrabhishek does not include Havan.

















Pooja Guideline

Sankalp
Sankalp is the most important part of any pooja. It includes your name, Gotra, and specific wishes, and is taken by our Panditji to ensure the spiritual benefits reach you. We follow true Vedic tradition, one Sankalp for one pooja at a time. We do not perform group Sankalps, as it goes against scriptural principles. Each pooja is done individually, with full focus and devotion.

Gotra
In every pooja or jaap, a Sankalp is taken with the devotee’s name, Gotra, and purpose. Gotra is not just a surname — it represents your ancestral lineage and connects your prayers to the Rishis and your entire family, both living and departed. If someone is unaware of their gotra, they can still perform the pooja or jaap, and they will receive the full benefits of the ritual. In such cases, the “Kashyap Gotra” will be used during the pooja.

Pooja Proof
We provide photos or short videos of key pooja moments like Sankalp or Abhishek, usually within 2 days. Full-length videos or live streaming are not possible. In rare cases (due to technical issues, power cuts, etc.), if proof cannot be shared, please rest assured that your pooja was sincerely performed with full Vedic rituals. Your official Temple Trust receipt confirms that the pooja was conducted properly in your name. We humbly seek your trust in our commitment to Dharma.

Pooja Schedule
Your payment is verified within 24 hours. After verification, the pooja is performed within the next 48 hours. In most cases, we complete the process earlier, but this is the maximum time we may require to ensure proper ritual preparation.

Pooja Samagri
All pooja materials are arranged by our temple pandit as per traditional rituals. The cost is included in the pooja charges. If something is unavailable, a suitable alternative is used.
